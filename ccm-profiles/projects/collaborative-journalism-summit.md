## Overview

The Collaborative Journalism Summit is an annual national conference about collaborative journalism, hosted by the Center for Cooperative Media. It brings together journalists, newsroom leaders, academics, and media stakeholders to share knowledge and strategies for collaborative journalism.

## History

The first Summit was held **May 4-5, 2017** at Montclair State University. It was presented by Google News Lab and the Rita Allen Foundation, and drew 175 people. Keynote presentations featured the International Consortium of Investigative Journalists on the Panama Papers and Google News Lab on the Electionland project.

At the inaugural Summit, the Center also announced its first open call for funding to support collaborative journalism projects (more than 70 applications for six grants of $7,000 each).

The 2026 Summit will be the **10th edition**, co-hosted with Temple University's Center for Community-Engaged Media (CCEM), directed by Letrell Crittenden.
