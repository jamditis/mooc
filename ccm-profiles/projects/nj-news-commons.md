## Overview

The NJ News Commons is the Center for Cooperative Media's flagship project — a broad network and coalition of organizations and people across New Jersey that work to provide and/or support the creation of quality news and information. It was born in the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy, when the Center began coordinating content sharing and collaborative activities among NJ news providers.

## Membership categories

- **Individual media membership** — Freelancers, academics, and employees affiliated with NJ-based news organizations, information platforms, or educational institutions.
- **Community stakeholder membership** — Individuals or organizations that aren't newsrooms but support the goal of growing and strengthening local news for NJ civic health.

## What members get

- **Communication and connection** — Members-only listserv, private Facebook group, industry newsletters (daily NJ news roundup, weekly newsletter, biweekly Newsroom Digest, biweekly collaborative journalism newsletter)
- **Coaching and counseling** — One-on-one support for ethical questions, legal referrals, grant proposal reviews, startup support
- **Content sharing** — Coordinated content sharing via Nordot story-sharing technology and partnerships with NJ Spotlight News
- **Translation services** — Spanish translation for news content (launched during the pandemic)
- **Ethnic and community media support** — Expanded outreach to diverse media outlets
- **Training and professional development** — Free skill-building (SEO, LinkedIn, podcasting, OPRA, etc.), plus scholarships for external programs
- **Awards and recognition** — Annual Excellence in Local News Awards (monetary prize)
- **Stipends** — Support for membership in professional associations (NABJ, NAHJ, AAJA, NLGJA, NAJA)
- **Peer groups** — Facilitated peer networks for partnership and collaboration
- **Shared photography and video** — Content pool available to members
- **Crisis planning and emergency funds** — Equipment purchase support during crises
- **NJ Media Mentors program** — Pairing former media professionals with current news leaders
- **Exclusive press briefings** — Organized for ethnic and community media members
