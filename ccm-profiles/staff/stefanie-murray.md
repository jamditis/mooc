## Background

Stefanie Murray is the director of the Center for Cooperative Media at Montclair State University and an advisor to the New Jersey Civic Information Consortium. A Michigan native, she previously worked in Michigan and Tennessee as a reporter, editor, digital media manager, and news executive.

Before joining Montclair State, she worked for Gannett Co. as vice president and executive editor of The Tennessean in Nashville and was with the Detroit Free Press before that.

## Education

- **M.S.** — Medill School of Journalism, Northwestern University
- **B.A.** — Broadcasting and journalism, Central Michigan University

## Role at CCM

As director, Stefanie leads the Center's overall strategy, manages relationships with funders including the Knight Foundation, Geraldine R. Dodge Foundation, and Democracy Fund, and serves as the primary spokesperson for CCM. She oversees three main program areas: the NJ News Commons, national collaborative journalism initiatives, and the Center's research agenda.

She also advises the NJ Civic Information Consortium and played a central role in securing the $2.5 million Knight Foundation grant in 2025 to launch the Collaborative Journalism Resource Hub.
