## Background

Manya Brachear Pashman is a journalist and educator who pioneered the religion and journalism dual master's program at Columbia University. She has over 15 years of experience reporting and editing stories with local, national, and international significance, and more than a decade of training aspiring journalists.

She implemented a nonprofit strategic plan for the Religion News Foundation and led efforts to diversify and professionalize its board. She has negotiated exclusive access and mined data for Page One stories and has discussed religion headlines in dozens of television and radio segments.

Currently, Manya writes about antisemitism around the globe, co-hosts a weekly podcast on global affairs through a Jewish lens, and teaches aspiring reporters how to cover religion responsibly.

She is fluent in most audio/video editing applications and all social media platforms.

## Role at CCM

Manya coordinates election-related projects for the Center, including initiatives like NJ Decides 2025 that provide free voter guide content and resources to NJ newsrooms for gubernatorial and legislative races.
