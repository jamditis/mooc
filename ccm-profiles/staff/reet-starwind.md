## Background

Reet Starwind is an artist, educator, and organizer in the Camden area. He graduated from Rutgers University-Camden in the field of digital media and society, and his career focuses on the connection between the two.

As Creative Director of the Camden nonprofit Watu Moja, he has curated showcases of local Black art and organized artistic demonstrations of civil unrest, such as the Camden Arts For Change Justice March. He has served as a featured speaker at events for the City of Camden, University of Pennsylvania, the All Things Are Possible Foundation, and Camden's Arts and Culture Heritage Society.

## Education

- **B.A.** — Digital media and society, Rutgers University-Camden

## Role at CCM

Reet coordinates the Stories Invincible project, working to elevate restorative narratives about Camden's residents and history. This project represents the Center's commitment to community-centered storytelling and amplifying voices from underserved areas of New Jersey.
