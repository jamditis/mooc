## Background

Amy Maestas has worked in journalism for more than 35 years as an editor, reporter, designer, and media executive. She was executive editor of The Durango Herald and other small newspapers and magazines in Southwest Colorado for a family-owned company, where she worked for 24 years. She was the digital director at The Salt Lake Tribune.

For five years, she worked at Solutions Journalism Network, directing the Local Media Project — a Knight Foundation-funded initiative that seeded and supported 15 solutions-focused journalism collaboratives across the U.S. She transitioned to CCM when the Collaborative Journalism Resource Hub was created.

## Fellowships

- Maynard Media Academy at the Nieman Foundation (2012)
- Knight-Wallace Fellow, University of Michigan (Class of 2017)

## Role at CCM

Amy directs the Collaborative Journalism Resource Hub, the centerpiece of a $2.5 million Knight Foundation grant. The Hub aims to support 20 journalism collaboratives across the U.S. over five years, providing up to $50,000 in financial support per collaborative plus coaching on revenue development and sustainability. The goal is to help collaboratives raise an additional $2.5M in local support. Amy brings continuity from the previous Knight-funded Local Media Project at Solutions Journalism Network.
