## Background

After working as a journalist and editor in the U.S. and abroad, Caroline Porter has spent the past decade modernizing business, product, and civic models for news and information: growing two start-ups, producing research for the field, and consulting for a mix of media companies, nonprofits, and academic organizations.

Key positions and roles:
- **Staff reporter** — The Wall Street Journal (Chicago and Los Angeles bureaus)
- **Instructor** — Reporting & Writing at Northwestern University's Medill School
- **Fulbright Scholar** — Northern Ireland (researched the role of media in the post-conflict region)
- **Director of impact and strategic partnerships** — National Trust for Local News
- **Principal** — Ralstin Agency (consultancy)
- **Director of product and strategic partnerships** — Open Campus

## Role at CCM

Caroline tracks and measures the impact of journalism collaboratives supported by the Hub. By the second year of operation, at least 50% of the collaboratives are expected to meet agreed-upon community impact metrics — Caroline's work is central to defining, tracking, and reporting on those outcomes.
